Spider Plant

Also known as

Anthericum comosum, Hartwegia comosa.

It is one of the easiest to grow houseplants, the Spider Plant is a low-maintenance, attractive addition to any home.

Steps to take care

  • Light: Spider plants thrive in bright, indirect light.
  • Temperature: Spider plants prefer average room temperatures between 60-75Â°F
  • Watering: Spider plants like to be evenly moist but not overly wet.
  • Fertilizer: Feed your spider plant with a balanced, water-soluble houseplant fertilizer.
  • Pest control: Spider plants are generally resistant to pests

How to take care

These plantlets can be rooted in water or soil to propagate new plants. Prune any brown or damaged leaves regularly to maintain the plant's appearance.

Fertilizers to use

Feed once every month during the spring and summer with an all-purpose fertilizer for indoor plants.

The Spider Plant is non-toxic and safe for humans, dogs, and cats.

While maintaining a garden is no child's play, it does not have to be nerve-wracking.
