Aloe Vera

Also Known As

Chinese aloe, Wand of heaven, Burn aloe.

Aloe Vera thrives in natural bright light, indoors or outdoors.

Steps to take care

  • Water sparingly
  • Allow the soil to dry out
  • Provide indirect sunlight
  • Wait for new growth
  • Transplant the new plant

How to care

Aloe Vera thrives in natural bright light, indoors or outdoors. However, direct harsh sunlight can cause damage. The plant also needs low watering.

You can water it once a week in summers and space the timing even more in winters and rainy seasons.

While maintaining a garden is no child's play, it does not have to be nerve-wracking.

Fertilizers to use

Enhanced resilience

Use about ¼ cup to ½ cup of fresh aloe vera per gallon of water total.
